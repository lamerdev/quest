Introduction

GemRaiders is an early public Alpha project found at GemRaiders.xyz. We blend dark-fantasy-inspired lore with a chat-driven dungeon crawler. This page explains the technical details of our stable release (v3) and the broader vision for building a blockchain-powered game. Visit us on Telegram at t.me/GemRaiders.

Bot & Technical Details

Our engine relies on a Telegram bot that handles players’ commands for forming parties, starting dungeons, tracking experience, awarding shards, and more. It also:

  • Keeps a JSON-based record (bot_data.json) of user stats, active dungeons, scenario scripts, etc.
  • Tracks each player's activity to mitigate spam and potential bot exploitation.
  • Updates user XP not only from combat but from chatting (within limits) and from completing scenarios or referrals.
  • Offers staminas (cooldowns) so players cannot spam multiple dungeons continuously without limit.
  • Maintains a flexible scene system: text scenes, combat scenes, boss scenes, etc. tied to scenario JSON files (e.g., dungeon1.json, dungeon2.json).

At this Alpha stage, we rely on a single “master” Python script (the “stable 3” version) with minimal external libraries. It organizes the entire dungeon logic (like /engage, /begin_dungeon, /next), enabling turn-based combat plus text-based scene progression.

We have anti-bot measures behind the scenes to detect suspicious spam or artificially inflated referral stats. While we won’t disclose the exact method, rest assured that an attempt to farm shards or XP automatically is unlikely to succeed long-term.

Gameplay Manual (All Commands)

Here’s a step-by-step guide to playing GemRaiders in detail. Note that one person in your party must decide which scenario to use (so discuss in chat who is picking).

1) Enter the Lobby

Use /dungeon_lobby to join the global lobby. You’ll see a list of who else is waiting. Up to 5 players can form one group.

2) Choose the Scenario

Any single party member picks the scenario with /begin_dungeon <num> after reviewing /list_dungeons. For instance: 

/list_dungeons
/begin_dungeon 2

The bot will form a new dungeon with its own internal ID (e.g. “Formed a party (ID=1).”). Everyone in the lobby is allocated to that new party.

3) Classes & Starting the Dungeon

Each player sets their class: /set_class Warrior (or Mage, Ranger, Healer). Then any party member can do /start_dungeon <ID>, e.g. /start_dungeon 1 if the bot said ID=1. You’ll see the first scene appear.

4) Text Scenes

Whenever a scene is type: text, you can type /next to move forward. Some text scenes also mention special events (like “everyone recovers 10 HP”).

5) Combat Scenes

For type: combat or type: boss, the bot will say “Enemies appear!” and ask you to /engage <ID>. Then the fight is turn-based:

  • /dattack <index|boss> (Deal damage. Warriors do 10, Mages 12, Healers 8, etc.)
  • /dskill heal (Healer only), adding +5 HP to self.
  • Inline attack buttons also appear each turn.

Once all mobs or the boss are defeated, you can /next to proceed. If you die, the run is lost.

Shards, Levels, Referral System

Shards

Shards are an in-game currency you earn by:

  • Completing dungeons successfully
  • Referral rewards (when your invitees become active)
  • Other in-game activities (story submissions, special events, etc.)

Soon, 100 Shards = 1 Gem. We plan to implement a specialized bot that lets you trade Shards for Gems. Gems may gain further utility (e.g., advanced content, unique items). This feature remains in development but should arrive in future updates.

Levels & XP

Players have an experience (XP) tally. You level up automatically when hitting certain XP thresholds. You gain XP from:

  • Posting messages in chat (up to a certain limit to prevent spam exploitation)
  • Finishing dungeons and defeating mobs or bosses
  • Referrals becoming active

Each level requires progressively more XP. Higher levels may unlock future perks or expansions (announced later).

Referrals

Each user has a personal invite link (/myinvite). When a new player starts via that link, you become their inviter. If they become active (surpassing a certain message threshold, etc.), you earn a Shard bonus. Inactive invitees do not yield bonus. The system tracks each user’s referrals, awarding Shards once they are deemed legitimate participants. Attempts to mass-invite bots or alt accounts are likely to be detected by our anti-bot mechanisms.

Stamina & Cooldowns

Each user has a stamina (attempt) limit for dungeons. Typically DAILY_DUNGEON_ATTEMPTS=1 in our current alpha. After each successful or failed run, your stamina decreases. We aim to maintain fairness and pace, ensuring one cannot spam runs infinitely in a short time.

Anti-Bot Protections

Our system monitors suspicious repeated patterns, flood attempts, or unnatural referral behavior. We do not disclose exact details to prevent circumvention, but rest assured: spamming or automated scripts are unlikely to gain Shards or XP effectively. We reserve the right to revoke illegitimate gains and block malicious accounts if detected.

Lore: Gem Finder, Shade & Eternal Observer

The GemRaiders universe follows the sagas of:

  • Gem Finder — A legendary traveler wearing a mantle studded with luminous gems, rumored to bridge hidden realities in pursuit of rare stones.
  • Shade — A tacit companion veiled in shadow, guiding the Finder and any parties through labyrinthine passages, safeguarding them from fatal traps and monstrous foes.
  • Eternal Observer — Our developer’s persona, maintaining a blog (in character) about the expansions, updates, and behind-the-scenes glimpses into the code and lore connections. This entity also acts as an archivist of daily logs and patch notes.

Our core environment is a dark-fantasy meets cryptic technology. Over time, we plan for deeper expansions connecting GemRaiders.xyz with day1.gemraiders.xyz, forging a cohesive multi-layered narrative of magic, relics, and beyond.
